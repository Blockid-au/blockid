### 1. What problem are you solving?

**Short version (for forms with character limits):**

Early-stage founders have no affordable way to know what their startup is worth. Manual valuations cost A$5,000-$50,000 and take weeks. Existing tools like Carta and Pulley manage cap tables but provide zero valuation intelligence. The result: 60% of Australian startups fail within 3 years, often because founders cannot articulate their value to investors, make informed equity decisions, or track their progress in any measurable way.

**Extended version:**

The startup ecosystem has a valuation black hole. In Australia alone, 437,150 new businesses start each year, but 370,500 close -- a 30.3% annual churn rate. The #1 cause of failure globally is running out of cash (70%, CB Insights), and a significant driver is that founders cannot accurately communicate their startup's value.

This is not just a convenience problem. Incorrect valuations lead to:
- **Over-dilution**: Founders give away too much equity too early because they undervalue their company
- **Failed fundraises**: Investors pass because founders cannot substantiate their ask
- **Misaligned cap tables**: Co-founder equity splits made on gut feeling lead to disputes and company death
- **Missed compliance**: Australian founders miss ESIC (Early Stage Innovation Company) eligibility, costing investors 20% tax offsets

The tools that exist (Carta, Pulley, Cake Equity) are cap table spreadsheets. None of them answer the fundamental question every founder asks: **"What is my startup actually worth?"**
