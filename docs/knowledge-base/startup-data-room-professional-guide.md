## 1. What Is a Professional Investor Data Room?

A startup data room is a secure, organized digital repository containing all documents and data that investors need to complete due diligence before making an investment decision. In 2026, best-in-class data rooms are:

- **Digital-native**: Hosted on platforms (not ZIP files or generic Google Drive)
- **Analytics-enabled**: Track who views what, for how long, and when
- **Access-controlled**: Individual investor links with NDA gating and permissions
- **Completeness-scored**: Show founders exactly what's missing
- **Stage-appropriate**: Pre-seed â‰  Series A requirements

## 2. The 10-Section Professional Structure

### Section 1: Corporate & Legal (CRITICAL)
**What investors verify:** Entity existence, governance, compliance
**Key AU documents:**
- ASIC Certificate of Registration
- ASIC Company Extract (<30 days old during active raises)
- Constitution or Replaceable Rules confirmation
- Register of Members (share register)
- Register of Directors & Secretaries
- Board minutes (last 12 months)

**Australian-specific:**
- Proprietary Limited (Pty Ltd) structure required
- ASIC Annual Review compliance
- ABN/ACN verification
- AUSTRAC registration if handling money transfers

### Section 2: Cap Table & Equity (CRITICAL)
**What investors verify:** Ownership clarity, founder alignment, no hidden claims
**Must include:**
- Full cap table (pre AND post-raise)
- All SAFEs/convertible notes on issue
- Founder vesting schedules (4yr/1yr cliff is AU standard)
- ESOP plan rules and register
- IP assignment deeds from all founders

**Red flags:**
- Missing IP assignment (biggest deal-killer)
- Misaligned founder vesting
- Undisclosed SAFEs or side letters
- Option pool not reflected in cap table

### Section 3: Financial Projections (CRITICAL)
**What investors verify:** Revenue model, unit economics, burn discipline
**Must include:**
- 3-year financial model (P&L, cash flow, balance sheet)
- Historical P&L for all operating periods
- Bank statements (6 months)
- Use of funds breakdown
- Multi-method valuation (SVI + revenue multiple + DCF + comps)

**Key metrics benchmarks (AU SaaS, 2026):**
- LTV:CAC > 3:1 for Series A
- Gross margin > 60% for software
- NRR > 100% for Series A
- Payback period < 12 months

### Section 4: Product & Technology (HIGH)
**What investors verify:** Product maturity, technical debt, scalability
**Key items:**
- Product demo video (most-viewed asset)
- Technical architecture document
- 12-18 month product roadmap
- Security posture (OWASP compliance, SSL A+)
- GitHub access (for technical VCs)

### Section 5: Market & Traction (CRITICAL)
**What investors verify:** Product-market fit, growth trajectory
**Key items:**
- Traction dashboard (live analytics preferred)
- Customer list with ACV values
- Market size analysis (bottom-up + top-down)
- Competitive differentiation matrix
- Google Analytics read-only access

### Section 6: Team & Advisors (CRITICAL)
**What investors verify:** Founder-market fit, execution capability
**Key items:**
- Founder bios with domain expertise evidence
- LinkedIn profiles (verified)
- Hiring plan tied to fundraise milestones
- Advisor agreements with equity/vesting

### Section 7: IP & Compliance (HIGH)
**Key items:**
- IP assignment deeds (every founder and contractor)
- Trademark/patent register
- Privacy Policy (Australian Privacy Act 1988 compliant)
- Regulatory licenses (AFSL, ACL, TGA if applicable)
- Open source license audit

### Section 8: Contracts & Agreements (HIGH)
**Key items:**
- Top 3 customer contracts by ACV
- Standard MSA template
- All employment agreements
- Advisor agreements
- Material vendor contracts

### Section 9: Strategy & Roadmap (HIGH)
**Key items:**
- Go-to-market strategy with channel costs
- Fundraise pipeline (investor names, stages, amounts)
- Exit strategy analysis with acquirer landscape
- 18-month milestone plan

### Section 10: References & Due Diligence (MEDIUM)
**Key items:**
- Pre-answered investor FAQ
- Press coverage / media mentions
- Awards and recognition (EMDG, R&D Tax, accelerators)
- Auto-generated DD checklist with completeness score

## 4. Investor Access Control Best Practices

### Access Token Types:
- **View-only**: Default for initial conversations
- **Download-enabled**: For serious leads after NDA
- **Section-restricted**: Share only financials with specific investors

### NDA Gating:
- Require NDA sign before viewing cap table and financial projections
- Track NDA signing events (IP, timestamp, name)
- Store signed NDA reference in data room

### Expiry:
- 30-day links for initial outreach
- 90-day links for active due diligence
- Never share permanent links

## 6. Australian Market Specific Notes

### Regulatory:
- **ASIC**: PPSR (Personal Property Securities Register) search required
- **ATO**: Verify R&D Tax Incentive claims are filed correctly
- **Privacy**: Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) compliance
- **EMDG**: Export grants should be documented

### Valuation (AU-Specific Discounts):
- AU startups typically valued at 20-30% discount to equivalent US deal
- Blackbird / AirTree typically model 20x+ returns for Series A
- AU pre-seed median: A$1.5-2.5M pre-money (2026 data)
- AU seed median: A$3-6M pre-money
- AU Series A median: A$10-20M pre-money

### Investor Types in Australia:
- **Micro-VCs**: Blackbird, AirTree, Square Peg, Airtree, Carthona, Folklore
- **Angels**: ASXN, Scale Investors, Sydney Angels, Melbourne Angels
- **Family Offices**: Macquarie, Ord Minnett, Perennial
- **Strategic**: Telstra Ventures, ANZ Ventures, Macquarie Capital
- **Government**: CSIRO, IP Australia, Austrade
