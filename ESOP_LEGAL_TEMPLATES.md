## Template 1: ESOP Share Plan Deed

```
ESOP SHARE PLAN DEED

THIS DEED made the ____ day of ___________ 20__

BETWEEN:

AUSCHAIN PTY LTD (ACN 659 615 111)
(hereinafter called "the Company")

- and -

[EMPLOYEE/PARTICIPANT NAME]
ABN/ACN: [if applicable]
(hereinafter called "the Participant")

RECITALS

WHEREAS the Board of Directors of the Company wishes to establish an 
Employee Share Option Plan ("the Plan") to:
(a) Encourage employees and service providers to acquire an interest in the Company
(b) Align employee/advisor interests with shareholder value creation
(c) Retain key talent through equity incentives

NOW IT IS HEREBY AGREED as follows:

### PART A: DEFINITIONS

1. "Option" means a right to acquire one Ordinary Share of the Company
2. "Strike Price" means A$0.10 per Option (or as specified in Grant Letter)
3. "Vesting Date" means the date from which Options become exercisable
4. "Cliff Date" means the 12-month anniversary of Grant Date
5. "Final Vesting Date" means 48 months from Grant Date
6. "Participant" means the employee/advisor identified above
7. "Company" means Auschain Pty Ltd (ACN 659 615 111)
8. "Board" means the Board of Directors of the Company

### PART B: GRANT & ALLOCATION

2. Grant Details
   2.1 The Company grants to the Participant, on the terms of this Deed:
       - Total Options Granted: _________ [e.g., 500]
       - Grant Date: _________________ [e.g., 2026-07-01]
       - Strike Price: A$0.10 per Option
       - Vesting Schedule: 4 years, 1-year cliff (see Section 3)
       
   2.2 This grant is personal to the Participant and non-transferable
   (except by will or court order in bankruptcy).
   
   2.3 Options are granted under Australian ESS concessions 
   (Income Tax Assessment Act 1997, Part 7A) and subject to this Deed.

### PART C: VESTING SCHEDULE

3. Vesting Terms
   
   3.1 Cliff Vesting (Month 0–12)
       - No Options vest during the first 12 months
       - If Participant leaves employment before Cliff Date, all unvested 
         Options are forfeited with no compensation
       - Vesting status on Cliff Date (Month 12): 25% of total grant
   
   3.2 Monthly Vesting (Month 13–48)
       - After Cliff Date, remaining 75% of Options vest monthly
       - Monthly vesting rate: 1.5625% of total grant per month
       - Formula: 75% ÷ 36 months = 1.5625% per month
       - Vesting occurs on the anniversary of Grant Date each month
       
   3.3 Vesting Example (500 options, Grant Date 2026-07-01)
       ```
       Cliff Date (12 months):    Month 12  = 125 vested (25%)
       Mid-point (24 months):     Month 24  = 312 vested (62%)
       Late-stage (36 months):    Month 36  = 437 vested (87%)
       Final Vesting (48 months): Month 48  = 500 vested (100%)
       ```

   3.4 Fractional Shares
       - Any fractional vesting is rounded down to the nearest whole share
       - Fractional remainder carries forward to next vesting date

### PART D: EXERCISE MECHANICS

4. Exercise of Options
   
   4.1 Vested Options may be exercised at any time after Vesting Date
       and before the 10-year anniversary of Grant Date (or upon termination).
   
   4.2 Exercise Process
       - Participant provides written notice (email acceptable) stating 
         number of Options to exercise
       - Payment method: [cash/cheque/bank transfer to Company]
       - Strike Price per Option: A$0.10
       - Total exercise cost = Number of Options × A$0.10
       - Company issues share certificate within 5 business days of payment
   
   4.3 No Loan Facility
       - Participant must pay Strike Price in full at exercise
       - Company may not provide non-recourse loans (optional feature)

### PART E: TERMINATION & LEAVER PROVISIONS

5. Leaver Provisions
   
   5.1 Good Leaver (Termination without cause or Resignation for good reason)
       - Participant may exercise vested Options within 90 days of termination
       - Unvested Options are forfeited (standard)
       - Company may optionally allow extension of exercise period
   
   5.2 Bad Leaver (Termination for cause / misconduct)
       - All Options (vested and unvested) are forfeited immediately
       - No compensation or repayment of any kind
       - Cause includes: theft, fraud, breach of confidentiality, etc.
   
   5.3 Death
       - Participant's estate may exercise vested Options within 12 months
       - Unvested Options continue vesting for 12 months post-death
         (or estate may choose to liquidate early)
   
   5.4 Disability / Retirement
       - If Participant becomes unable to work (>50% permanent impairment):
         All remaining Options accelerate and become immediately exercisable
       - At 65+ age retirement: Vesting continues for 12 additional months

### PART F: ACCELERATION CLAUSES (OPTIONAL)

6. Acceleration Events
   
   6.1 Change of Control
       - If the Company is acquired / merged / IPO'd at valuation >10x 
         current post-money valuation, all unvested Options immediately vest
       - Trigger applies 90 days before transaction close
   
   6.2 Double-Trigger Acceleration (Acquirer Termination)
       - If acquired and Participant is terminated within 12 months 
         without cause: 50% of unvested Options accelerate
       - Protects employees in M&A scenarios
   
   6.3 Board Discretion
       - Board may accelerate vesting for retention purposes, with 
         Participant's written consent

### PART G: TAXATION & COMPLIANCE

7. Tax Matters
   
   7.1 ESS Concessions
       - This Plan is designed to comply with Australian ESS concessions
         (ITAA 1997 Part 7A)
       - Options must be granted at/above Fair Market Value (A$0.10)
       - Participant may be eligible for concessional CGT if held 5+ years
   
   7.2 Taxation Obligation
       - Participant is responsible for all taxation arising from this grant
       - Taxation may occur at:
         (a) Grant date (if FMV >Strike Price; typically nil)
         (b) Exercise date (taxable benefit = FMV - Strike × number)
         (c) Sale date (Capital Gains Tax on appreciation)
       - Company will provide tax documentation; Participant to seek own advice
   
   7.3 Fringe Benefits Tax
       - Options (unexercised) do not trigger FBT
       - Post-exercise: FBT may apply if Options given to employees 
         for below-market value (Participant liable)
   
   7.4 Company Deduction
       - Company may claim tax deduction for option expense 
         (timing determined by tax law)

### PART H: RESTRICTIONS & CONDITIONS

8. Restricted Dealings
   
   8.1 Non-Transferability
       - Options are personal to Participant
       - Cannot be sold, gifted, or pledged to third parties
       - Exception: Transfer by will on death
   
   8.2 Voting & Dividends
       - Unvested Options carry no voting rights
       - Once exercised, Shares carry full voting rights
       - Dividends paid if declared by Board (on vested-and-exercised shares)
   
   8.3 Confidentiality & IP Assignment
       - Participant warrants that all work performed is confidential 
         and properly assigned to Company
       - Any breach of confidentiality / IP assignment may trigger 
         immediate forfeiture (bad leaver provision)

### PART I: ADMINISTRATION & DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9. Plan Administration
   
   9.1 Plan Committee
       - Board of Directors administers the Plan
       - CEO/CFO maintains cap table and vesting records
       - Participant may request vesting statement annually
   
   9.2 Amendments
       - Company may amend Plan terms with Participant's written consent
       - Force-modification of vesting is not permitted 
         (Participant's vested balance is protected)
   
   9.3 Governing Law
       - This Deed is governed by the laws of Victoria, Australia
       - Disputes resolved via Australian courts 
         (or arbitration if agreed)

### PART J: SIGNATURES

By signing below, both parties agree to the terms of this ESOP Share Plan Deed.

**PARTICIPANT:**

___________________________________  Date: ______________
[Participant Name & Signature]

**COMPANY:**

___________________________________  Date: ______________
[CEO / Authorized Director]

Printed Name: _________________________

## Template 3: Founder Vesting Confirmation Deed

```
FOUNDER SHARE VESTING CONFIRMATION DEED

THIS DEED made the 13th day of June 2026

BETWEEN:

DO VAN LONG
(hereinafter called "the Founder")

- and -

AUSCHAIN PTY LTD (ACN 659 615 111)
(hereinafter called "the Company")

RECITALS

WHEREAS:

A. The Founder is the sole shareholder of the Company
B. The Company's Board recognizes the Founder's work from incorporation
C. The Board wishes to issue founder shares with retroactive vesting 
   to align with investor expectations
D. Standard founder vesting (4-year, 1-year cliff) demonstrates best practice

NOW IT IS AGREED:

1. FOUNDER SHARE ISSUANCE

   1.1 The Company hereby issues to the Founder:
       - Number of Shares: 88,000
       - Strike Price: A$0.0001 per share (nominal)
       - Total Consideration: A$8.80 (nominal, to be paid by offset of past services)
   
   1.2 Founder warrants that:
       - All work performed has been done on behalf of the Company
       - All intellectual property created vests with the Company
       - Founder has no claim for additional compensation

2. RETROACTIVE VESTING SCHEDULE

   2.1 Founder Shares shall vest retroactively as follows:
       - Vesting Commencement Date: 1 January 2024 (incorporation date)
       - Cliff Date: 1 January 2025 (12 months)
       - Final Vesting Date: 1 January 2028 (48 months)
   
   2.2 As of 13 June 2026 (today):
       - Period elapsed: 2.5 years
       - Vesting status: ~65% vested (approximately 57,200 shares)
       - Remaining unvested: ~35% (approximately 30,800 shares)
       - Cliff surpassed: Yes (1-year cliff fully satisfied)
   
   2.3 Post-Vesting Dates:
       - 1 January 2027: 75% vested
       - 1 January 2028: 100% vested (fully vested)

3. CONSEQUENCES

   3.1 If Founder leaves before 1 January 2028:
       - Founder may retain vested shares (~65% currently)
       - Unvested shares (~35%) revert to ESOP pool
       - No compensation for forfeited shares
   
   3.2 If Founder continues to 1 January 2028:
       - All 88,000 shares fully vested
       - No additional vesting schedules required

4. TAX IMPLICATIONS

   4.1 Shares issued at nominal strike (A$0.0001) = minimal tax impact
   4.2 Founder may have assessable income = fair market value - strike
   4.3 As of June 2026, FMV ~ A$0.10, so tax liability ~ A$0.10 × 88,000 = A$8,800
   4.4 This is ONE-TIME tax (not recurring), likely deferred to FY 2026-27
   4.5 Founder to seek tax advice on optimal timing/treatment

5. CAP TABLE IMPACT

   5.1 Before this Deed:
       - Do Van Long: 100 shares (100%)
       
   5.2 After this Deed:
       - Do Van Long: 88,000 shares (88%)
       - ESOP Pool: 12,000 shares (12%)
       - Total: 100,000 shares (100%)
   
   5.3 Share split is 1,000:1 for accounting/fractional clarity

6. GOVERNANCE

   6.1 This Deed creates vesting terms for transparency with future investors
   6.2 Founder acknowledges that all future hires will have similar 4yr/1yr terms
   6.3 Founder's vesting terms align with market best practice
   6.4 This Deed protects the Company and Founder in case of investor due diligence

7. SIGNATURES

By signing below, Founder and Company acknowledge and accept this Vesting Confirmation.

**FOUNDER:**

_______________________________  Date: ______________
Do Van Long

**COMPANY (by Board Resolution):**

_______________________________  Date: ______________
Do Van Long, CEO

## Template 5: Shareholders Agreement (Extract)

```
SHAREHOLDERS AGREEMENT — EQUITY & VESTING CLAUSE

### VESTING SCHEDULE (for all option holders)

5.1 All options granted under the ESOP Plan shall vest as follows:
    - Cliff: 25% after 12 months of employment
    - Monthly: Remaining 75% over 36 months (1.5625%/month)
    - Total Period: 48 months

5.2 Leaver Provisions:
    - Good Leaver: May exercise vested options within 90 days
    - Bad Leaver: All options forfeited immediately
    - Death: Estate has 12 months to exercise

5.3 Acceleration Events:
    - Change of Control (>10x valuation): 100% immediate vesting
    - Acquirer Termination: 50% acceleration within 12 months post-close
    - Board may approve other acceleration with shareholder consent

5.4 Dilution Protection (Anti-dilution rights):
    - If future rounds occur at lower valuation, option holders' strike price 
      may be reduced to weighted average (standard dilution adjustment)
    - Protects employees from down-rounds

5.5 Drag-Along / Tag-Along:
    - If founder + investors vote to sell Company:
      All option holders must participate (drag-along)
    - If founder is selling >50%, option holders can join sale (tag-along)
```
