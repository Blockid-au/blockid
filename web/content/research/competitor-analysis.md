# BlockID.au Competitor Analysis — AU Cap Table & Equity Management

**Compiled: 2026-05-19 | CMO Week 1 Research**

## Executive Summary

BlockID.au operates in the Australian startup equity management space with a differentiated position: AI-powered valuation intelligence combined with cap table tools, built natively for Australian founders. The five primary competitors analysed below range from US-dominant incumbents (Carta, Pulley) to Asia-Pacific players (Qapita) and closer regional rivals (Cake Equity, Vestd). BlockID's unique advantages include AI-driven Startup Value Index (SVI) scoring, ASIC/ATO-native compliance, free-tier tools, and an integrated insights content strategy — none of which competitors match in combination.

---

## 1. Carta (carta.com)

### Overview
- **HQ:** San Francisco, USA
- **Founded:** 2012
- **Market position:** Dominant global cap table platform; end-to-end equity management for private companies and funds
- **Valuation:** ~$7.4B (2021 round; subsequent down-round to ~$3B in 2023)

### Pricing
| Plan | Price (USD/yr) | Stakeholders | Key Features |
|------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Up to 25 | Basic cap table, recently incorporated only, <$1M raised |
| Launch | $280 | Up to 25 | Electronic securities, basic compliance |
| Starter | $2,800 | Up to 50 | 409A valuations, board management, scenario modelling |
| Growth | ~$6,000+ | Up to 100 | Advanced reporting, equity plan admin |
| Enterprise | $6K-$77K+ | Custom | Full suite, fund admin, liquidity |

### AU Compliance
- **Yes, expanding.** Carta has built specific AU features:
  - Digital ASIC share register management (allotments + transfers)
  - ESS (Employee Share Scheme) stock options replacing generic "international" securities
  - ATO-compatible equity compensation reporting
  - Australia's first integrated ESS compliance suite (per their own release notes)
- Source: [Carta AU Product Updates](https://carta.com/uk/en/product-updates/international-features-cap-table/), [ESS Compliance Suite](https://releasenotes.carta.com/introducing-australia-s-first-integrated-ess-compliance-suite-1P79Ju)

### AI Capabilities
- **Limited.** No public AI-powered valuation or scoring tools. 409A valuations are analyst-driven (not AI). No equivalent to BlockID's SVI.

### Weaknesses
1. **Pricing opacity** — Enterprise costs range $6K-$77K; unpredictable for early-stage founders
2. **US-first design** — AU features are bolt-ons, not native; Australian regulatory nuances may be secondary
3. **No free valuation tool** — 409A valuations require paid plans ($2,800+/yr minimum)
4. **Data privacy concerns** — US-hosted data; no AU data residency guarantees
5. **Reputation risk** — 2023 controversy over data handling (selling customer data to investors); trust deficit
6. **No content/insights engine** — No equivalent to BlockID's SEO-driven founder education content
7. **Overkill for pre-seed** — Feature complexity unsuitable for idea-stage founders

### BlockID Advantage
BlockID offers free AU-native cap table, AI-powered valuation (SVI), and pre-diligence tools at $0-$49 vs Carta's $280-$2,800+ entry. BlockID is purpose-built for Australian regulatory context from day one.

## 2. Pulley (pulley.com)

### Overview
- **HQ:** San Francisco, USA
- **Founded:** 2019
- **Market position:** Modern cap table challenger to Carta; YC-backed
- **Focus:** Simplicity and developer-friendly equity management

### Pricing
| Plan | Price (USD/yr) | Stakeholders | Key Features |
|------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Startup | $1,200 | Up to 25 | Cap table, share certs, SAFE/Option/RSA agreements |
| Growth | $3,500 | Up to 40 | Compliance reporting (ASC 718), custom integrations |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Liquidity solutions, dedicated support |
| Token Cap Table | $4,500 | Up to 25 | Crypto/token management |
| 409A Valuation | From $10,000 | - | IRS-compliant valuations |

### AU Compliance
- **None.** No mention of Australian jurisdiction support, ASIC compliance, or ATO reporting anywhere on the platform.

### AI Capabilities
- **None.** No AI features mentioned in product documentation or marketing.

### Weaknesses
1. **No Australian support** — Zero AU compliance features; not suitable for Australian companies
2. **High entry price** — $1,200/yr minimum vs BlockID's free tier
3. **US-centric** — SOC 2 Type II compliance but no international regulatory support
4. **Stakeholder caps** — Hard limits require sales contact for larger orgs
5. **No valuation intelligence** — Valuations start at $10K (analyst-driven, not AI)
6. **No content strategy** — No founder education resources

### BlockID Advantage
Pulley has zero AU relevance. BlockID is the clear choice for any Australian founder; Pulley is only relevant if an AU startup has US investors demanding a US platform.

## 3. Qapita (qapita.com)

### Overview
- **HQ:** Singapore
- **Founded:** 2019
- **Market position:** Leading Asia-Pacific equity management platform
- **Revenue:** SGD 8.36M (March 2025)
- **Funding:** Series B (October 2025)
- **Clients:** 2,000+ companies; 300,000+ employees; $55B+ equity value managed

### Pricing
| Plan | Price (USD/yr) | Stakeholders | Key Features |
|------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Spark | Free | Up to 25 | Basic cap table, equity plan admin, <$1M funding |
| Surge | $1,600 | Up to 40 (+$40/stakeholder) | Reporting, scenario modelling |
| Growth | $3,000 | Up to 50 (+$60/stakeholder) | 409A valuations, board consents |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Full-service, premium features |

### AU Compliance
- **Minimal.** Qapita supports "multiple jurisdictions" for financial reporting but makes no explicit mention of ASIC, ATO, or Australian-specific regulatory alignment. Primary focus is India + Southeast Asia (Singapore, Vietnam, Thailand).

### AI Capabilities
- **None mentioned.** No AI features in product documentation.

### Weaknesses
1. **Not AU-focused** — Primary markets are India and SEA; Australia is an afterthought
2. **Several features "Coming Soon"** — Form 3921, Rule 701, custom reports still in development
3. **Per-stakeholder pricing** — Costs escalate quickly for growing startups
4. **No Australian templates** — Lacks AU-specific legal documents (ASIC share register, ESS)
5. **No valuation intelligence** — No AI-powered scoring or assessment tools
6. **Limited brand awareness in AU** — Not known in Australian founder community

### BlockID Advantage
Qapita is the closest regional competitor but lacks AU compliance depth. BlockID's ASIC/ATO-native tools, AI valuation, and Australian content strategy create a significant moat. Qapita may become relevant if they expand to AU — worth monitoring.

## 4. Cake Equity (cakeequity.com)

### Overview
- **HQ:** Australia (Sydney)
- **Founded:** 2018
- **Market position:** Australia's most established native cap table platform
- **Focus:** Cap table management, ESOP administration, equity compliance for AU startups

### Pricing
| Plan | Price (AUD/yr) | Stakeholders | Key Features |
|------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Up to 5 | Basic cap table, stock options, SAFE notes |
| Starter | ~$0 base + $1/stakeholder | Up to 25 | Vesting, SAFEs, templated option grants, benchmarking |
| Growth | ~$0 base + $5/stakeholder | Up to 30 | 409A valuation, incentive plans, contract editor, options exercising |
| Pro | Custom | Custom | ESS/ASC 718 reporting, international pools, bare trusts, priority support |
| 409A Add-on | $1,500 | - | Available on any plan |

### AU Compliance
- **Strong.** This is Cake's primary differentiator:
  - AUD currency support with GST calculations
  - Australian stock option plan legal templates
  - Bare trust support (relevant for AU tax structuring)
  - Country selector includes AU and NZ
  - ESS reporting (locked behind Pro tier)
  - However, advanced AU compliance features require the custom-priced Pro plan

### AI Capabilities
- **None.** No AI features mentioned anywhere in their platform or marketing materials.

### Weaknesses
1. **Opaque pricing** — Starter and Growth show "$0 + GST" without revealing actual base costs; confusing
2. **Limited free tier** — Only 5 stakeholders vs BlockID's broader free offering
3. **No AI/valuation intelligence** — No SVI equivalent; no automated scoring or AI-powered insights
4. **ESS reporting locked behind Pro** — Key AU compliance feature requires custom (expensive) plan
5. **Limited reporting** — Users report reporting features feel limited and inflexible
6. **No content/SEO strategy** — No founder education blog or insights engine comparable to BlockID
7. **No investor-readiness tools** — No pre-diligence, no data room builder, no term sheet analyser
8. **Single-focus product** — Cap table only; no broader startup intelligence platform

### BlockID Advantage
Cake is BlockID's most direct competitor. However, BlockID offers a fundamentally different value proposition: AI-powered valuation intelligence + cap table + pre-diligence + investor-readiness scoring + 8 free tools + 30+ SEO articles. Cake offers cap table management alone. BlockID must position against Cake as "the platform you grow into" vs "just a spreadsheet replacement."

## 5. Vestd (vestd.com)

### Overview
- **HQ:** United Kingdom
- **Founded:** 2014
- **Market position:** UK's leading equity management platform; FCA-authorised, B-Corp, ISO 27001 certified
- **Focus:** UK employee share schemes (EMI, CSOP, growth shares)

### Pricing
| Plan | Price (GBP/mo) | Key Features |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Startup | From GBP 25 | Basic equity management, up to 10 recipients |
| Scaleup/SME | From GBP 155 | Full share scheme admin, Companies House integration |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom support, multiple schemes |
- Minimum 12-month subscription for all plans
- Up to 4 share scheme valuations per year included

### AU Compliance
- **None.** Exclusively UK-focused:
  - Two-way Companies House integration (UK only)
  - EMI (Enterprise Management Incentives) — UK tax scheme
  - CSOP (Company Share Option Plan) — UK tax scheme
  - No ASIC, ATO, or Australian regulatory support of any kind

### AI Capabilities
- **None mentioned.** No AI features in their platform.

### Weaknesses
1. **UK-only** — Zero Australian relevance; cannot support AU company structures
2. **No international expansion** — Deeply tied to UK Companies House and HMRC
3. **No valuation intelligence** — Valuations are manual, limited to 4/year
4. **Not a direct competitor** — Only relevant as a benchmark for what a jurisdiction-specific equity platform looks like done well

### BlockID Advantage
Vestd is not a direct competitor but serves as an aspirational model: a jurisdiction-native platform that deeply integrates with local regulators (Companies House). BlockID should aim to be "the Vestd of Australia" — deeply integrated with ASIC and ATO, with the added layer of AI intelligence.

## Strategic Recommendations

### Immediate Positioning (Week 1-4)
1. **"Australia's AI Equity Platform"** — No competitor offers AI-powered valuation intelligence. This is BlockID's primary differentiator.
2. **Anti-Carta messaging** — Position against Carta's opacity, US-centricity, and privacy concerns. "Built in Sydney, for Sydney founders."
3. **Free tool superiority** — 8 free tools vs Cake's 1 free tool (5-holder cap table). Lead with generosity.
4. **Content moat** — 30+ SEO articles vs competitors' minimal content. Accelerate to 50+ to dominate AU startup search.

### Key Battleground: Cake Equity
Cake is the only direct AU competitor. Win by:
- Offering more free functionality (8 tools vs 1)
- AI-powered insights (SVI, term sheet analysis) that Cake cannot match
- Transparent pricing vs Cake's opaque "$0 + GST" structure
- Broader platform vision (valuation + equity + compliance + matching)

### Moat Expansion
- Deepen ASIC/ATO integrations (follow Vestd's Companies House model)
- Build API for accountants and lawyers to embed BlockID tools
- Pursue SOC 2 and ISO 27001 to match Carta/Vestd trust signals
- Consider Qapita partnership or monitoring — they may expand to AU
