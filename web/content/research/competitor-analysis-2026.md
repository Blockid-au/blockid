## Individual Competitor Profiles

### 1. Carta

**Overview:** The market leader in cap table and equity management globally. US-headquartered with a dominant position in the US venture-backed startup ecosystem. Serves from Launch (free) through to public companies and fund administrators.

**Pricing:**
- **Launch** -- Free (up to 25 stakeholders, raised less than $1M)
- **Build** -- ~$3,000-6,000/yr (early-stage)
- **Grow** -- ~$6,000-12,000/yr (growth-stage)
- **Scale** -- Custom pricing (late-stage / pre-IPO)
- Per-stakeholder fees apply on top of base platform fee

**Key Features:**
- Full cap table management with shareholder register
- 409A valuations (unlimited, customised by stage)
- ASC 820 valuations, Rule 701 management
- Fund administration for VCs
- Board meeting management
- Form 3921 generation
- Audit-based expense accounting (ASC 718)
- Liquidity programs and secondary transactions

**Target Market:** US venture-backed companies from seed to IPO. Fund administrators and institutional investors.

**Australia Presence:** No Australian entity or AU-specific compliance features. US-centric pricing, legal templates, and tax frameworks. Australian startups using Carta must manage AU compliance separately.

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive for early-stage startups (pricing escalates quickly)
- Not intuitive for non-specialists -- requires knowledge of securities law
- No bulk-update capability for option grants
- No Australian ESS compliance or ASIC integration
- US-centric legal templates do not cover AU corporate structures
- Salary compensation tool is an expensive add-on
- Has faced trust issues after a 2024 controversy around secondary data usage

**BlockID Positioning Angle:** Carta does not serve the AU market natively. BlockID can win pre-seed/seed AU founders who need local compliance, AI-powered valuation, and transparent ownership -- all without US lock-in.

### 2. Pulley

**Overview:** A modern Carta alternative built for venture-backed startups. Known for cleaner UX, lower pricing, and strong SAFE/convertible note modeling. Growing rapidly as a cost-effective choice for seed to Series C companies.

**Pricing:**
- **Startup** -- $1,200/yr (25 stakeholders included)
- **Growth** -- $3,500/yr (40 stakeholders included)
- **Enterprise** -- Custom pricing
- Also offers crypto/token cap table plans ($4,500/yr)

**Key Features:**
- Cap table management with share certificates
- Templated SAFE, option, and RSA agreements
- 409A valuations (Growth tier+)
- Fundraise modeler with dilution scenarios
- Interactive offer letters for recruiting
- Option exercises, Rule 701, Form 3921
- Board approvals and governance records
- HRIS integrations
- ASC 718 stock-based compensation reporting (Enterprise)

**Target Market:** Venture-backed or venture-aspiring startups, Delaware C-corps, Seed to Series C. Teams with equity-heavy compensation.

**Australia Presence:** None. US-only legal framework. No AU tax compliance, no ASIC integration.

**Weaknesses:**
- US-only legal framework (Delaware C-corp focus)
- No Australian ESS or tax-deferred ESOP support
- Smaller ecosystem than Carta (fewer integrations, smaller network)
- No AI-powered features
- No Australian entity or local support
- Limited to equity management -- no valuation intelligence or startup scoring

**BlockID Positioning Angle:** Pulley is a better Carta for US startups, but irrelevant to AU founders who need local compliance. BlockID offers what Pulley cannot: AI valuation, AU-native compliance, and a trust layer from day one.

### 3. Qapita

**Overview:** Singapore-headquartered equity management platform serving APAC startups and listed companies. Offers cap table, ESOP management, valuations, and a secondary marketplace. Strongest APAC competitor to Carta.

**Pricing:**
- **Spark** -- Free (up to 25 stakeholders, <$1M raised)
- **Surge** -- $1,600/yr (40 stakeholders, +$40 per additional/yr)
- **Growth** -- Custom (adds valuations: 409A, ESOP/RSU, IFRS 2)
- **Enterprise** -- Custom (full-service equity admin, liquidity programs)
- Add-ons: 409A valuation ($1,500), townhalls ($500/hr), analyst support ($125/hr)

**Key Features:**
- Cap table management with timestamped audit trail
- ESOP administration (grant creation, vesting, employee visibility)
- Scenario modeling and data room
- DocuSign integration (Surge+)
- Board consents and custom reporting
- Multiple valuation types (409A, ESOP, convertible, warrant, PPA)
- IFRS 2 expense reporting
- Liquidity programs and secondary marketplace
- Corporate actions management

**Target Market:** APAC startups from seed to IPO. Singapore, India, and Southeast Asia primary markets. Expanding into Australia.

**Australia Presence:** APAC-headquartered but not AU-native. No specific Australian ESS compliance, no ASIC integration. General APAC positioning rather than AU-first.

**Weaknesses:**
- UI/UX described as needing improvement by users
- Limited customization options
- E-sign feature reported as laggy
- Poor online documentation and limited self-service support resources
- Onboarding requires significant effort without enough guides/videos
- Not AU-native -- lacks Australian tax and compliance specifics
- Feature bloat reported by some users ("too many features")

**BlockID Positioning Angle:** Qapita is the closest APAC competitor, but it is Singapore-first, not Australia-first. BlockID can differentiate with AI-powered valuation (Qapita has none), Australian ESS/ASIC compliance roadmap, and a simpler UX for early-stage founders.

### 4. Cake Equity

**Overview:** The leading Australian-built cap table and equity management platform. Default choice for AU and APAC startups. Strong on cross-border ESOP compliance (AU ESS, US 83(b), UK EMI). Most direct competitor to BlockID in the Australian market.

**Pricing:**
- **Free** -- $0 (5 stakeholders, core cap table)
- **Starter** -- $0/yr (25 stakeholders, +$1 per additional)
- **Growth** -- $0/yr (30 stakeholders, +$5 per additional) -- most popular
- **Pro** -- Custom pricing (large cap tables, advanced compliance)
- Add-on: 409A valuation ($1,500)
- Note: Cake advertises "no hidden fees, no surprise annual increases, no 2-3 year lock-in"

**Key Features:**
- Cap table management with instant setup
- Stock options, RSUs, RSAs, SAFE notes, convertible notes, warrants
- Australian ESS reporting and tax-deferred ESOP support
- Cross-border equity: AU ESS, US 83(b), UK EMI
- Fundraising / scenario modeling
- Equity benchmarking for teams
- Integrated e-signing
- Shareholder and employee portals
- Board approvals and governance
- Option exercising workflows
- IFRS 2 / ASC 718 share-based payment reporting (Pro)
- Bare trusts support
- Free basic migration

**Target Market:** Australian and APAC startups from seed to Series B. Companies with cross-border teams needing multi-jurisdiction ESOP compliance.

**Australia Presence:** Native Australian company. Strongest AU compliance features in the market. Default choice for AU startups referred by accelerators and VCs.

**Weaknesses:**
- Focused purely on cap table and equity management -- no AI valuation or startup scoring
- No investor readiness or pre-diligence features
- No blockchain or trust verification layer
- Limited to equity management -- does not address the broader "prove your startup value" problem
- Growth tier is popular but per-stakeholder costs add up at scale
- No idea valuation or scenario planning beyond cap table dilution
- Limited thought leadership content compared to Carta/Pulley

**BlockID Positioning Angle:** Cake Equity is the cap table tool. BlockID is the valuation intelligence platform. They are complementary, not directly competitive today. BlockID wins in the "pre-cap-table" phase: idea validation, SVI scoring, AI-powered investor readiness. As BlockID builds cap table features, the overlap increases -- but the AI valuation + blockchain trust layer remains a unique moat. Consider a Cake Equity integration partnership.

### 5. Orchestra (formerly Orchestrate)

**Overview:** New Zealand-headquartered equity management platform serving NZ and AU startups. Focused on share registries, ESOP management, investor relations, and compliance. Smaller player but strong in the ANZ early-stage ecosystem.

**Pricing:**
- **Essentials** -- $2/stakeholder/month (share register, investor dashboards)
- **Engagement** -- $5/ESOP participant/month (ESOP pool management)
- **Enterprise** -- Custom pricing (125+ stakeholders)
- Minimum $20/month

**Key Features:**
- Share registry management
- Cap table management
- Employee share schemes (ESOP)
- Investor portal with document vault
- Automated dividend calculations and distribution
- Stakeholder communications
- Companies Office compliance (NZ-focused)

**Target Market:** Early-stage NZ and AU startups. Companies wanting simple, affordable equity management without US-centric complexity.

**Australia Presence:** Serves AU startups but NZ-headquartered. NZ Companies Office compliance is strong; AU ASIC compliance is less developed.

**Weaknesses:**
- Very small player -- hundreds of customers vs. Carta's tens of thousands
- NZ-first, AU-second
- No AI features
- No valuation capabilities
- No scenario modeling or fundraising tools
- Limited integrations
- No US compliance (409A, etc.) -- purely ANZ focused
- Minimal brand awareness in AU market

**BlockID Positioning Angle:** Orchestra is a lightweight ANZ equity tool. BlockID can absorb this market segment by offering everything Orchestra does plus AI valuation, startup scoring, and a trust layer. Orchestra's small scale makes it a non-threatening competitor.

## Strategic Summary

### BlockID Competitive Advantages (Unique to Market)

1. **AI-Powered Valuation Intelligence** -- No competitor offers AI-driven startup scoring or valuation. Carta and Pulley have scenario modeling, but nothing like SVI.
2. **Australia-Native** -- Only Cake Equity matches this. Carta, Pulley, and Qapita are foreign platforms.
3. **Pre-Cap-Table Value Proposition** -- BlockID serves founders before they even need a cap table (idea validation, SVI scoring). Every competitor assumes you already have a cap table.
4. **Blockchain Trust Layer** -- No competitor offers verified ownership or trust verification on-chain.
5. **AI-Agent Built Platform** -- Built with AI agents in 19 days. This is a marketing story no competitor can tell.

### Key Threats

1. **Cake Equity** is the most dangerous competitor -- Australian-native, free tier, strong ESOP compliance, established VC referral network. If Cake adds AI features, they become a direct threat.
2. **Qapita** is expanding into Australia from APAC. Their VC backing and marketplace features could gain traction.
3. **Carta** could enter AU market by acquiring a local player (e.g., Cake Equity).

### Recommended Actions

1. **Position as "AI valuation platform" not "cap table tool"** -- avoids direct comparison with Cake/Carta
2. **Build Cake Equity integration** -- complement rather than compete in the short term
3. **Target pre-seed/seed founders** before they choose a cap table tool -- own the "idea to first round" journey
4. **Leverage the Founding 50 program** as a wedge against Cake's free tier
5. **Publish AU-specific content** on ESIC, ESS, ASIC compliance to build SEO authority that US competitors cannot match
