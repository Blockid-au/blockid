## Why Australian Startups Need a Different Data Room Approach

Australian capital markets have AU-specific compliance requirements that international data room templates miss. A generic Silicon Valley template will not cover ASIC company documents, R&D Tax Incentive claims, BAS reconciliation, or ESIC eligibility — all of which are scrutinised by Australian investors. Every section of your data room should be built with AU-specific requirements in mind.

## Section 1: Company Formation and ASIC Documents

This section answers a single question: does this company legally exist, and is it properly structured?

**Required documents:**
- ASIC Certificate of Registration
- Company Constitution (or written confirmation of replaceable rules adoption)
- Current ASIC Company Extract (company history, name changes, director history)
- ABN registration confirmation
- GST registration (mandatory above A$75K annual revenue)
- Evidence of annual ASIC review fee payments (last 2 years)

**AU-specific note for international investors:** Include a one-paragraph note in each section explaining the Australian equivalent of any document. "ASIC is Australia's corporate regulator, equivalent to Companies House (UK) or the Delaware Secretary of State (US)." International VCs participating in syndicated AU rounds appreciate this context.

**Stage benchmark:** This section should be 100% complete before any investor conversation. No exceptions.

## Section 2: Cap Table and Equity Structure

The cap table is the single most scrutinised document in Australian due diligence. Investors need to see exactly who owns what, under what terms, and what the post-investment structure looks like.

**Required documents:**
- Fully diluted cap table (current and post-investment scenarios)
- Founder share certificates
- Shareholder register (current, ASIC-stamped)
- Shareholders Agreement (SHA) — signed by all parties
- Founder vesting schedules
- SAFE or convertible note agreements (with conversion mechanics modelled)
- ESIC eligibility documentation (if applicable — critical for investor tax offset claims)

**The ESIC opportunity:** If your company qualifies as an **Early Stage Innovation Company (ESIC)**, your investors are entitled to a 20% tax offset on their investment. This is a material commercial differentiator when pitching to individual angels and family offices. Document ESIC eligibility explicitly — include an ATO confirmation letter and the eligibility checklist.

**Common gap:** SHA signed by founders but not by all historical angels or early investors. This is a legal landmine. Resolve it before opening the data room.

## Section 3: ESOP Documentation

Your ESOP deserves its own section. Investors at seed and Series A spend significant time on ESOP structure because a non-compliant or undersized pool creates dilution problems post-investment.

**Required documents:**
- ESS deed (complete, board-approved, with execution date)
- Individual option agreements for all current optionholders
- Option holder register (separate from shareholder register)
- ATO ESS Annual Report lodgements
- ESOP pool summary: total size, granted, vested, unvested, available
- Good leaver / bad leaver provisions summary

**Stage benchmark:** At seed, a compliant deed and at least 2–3 signed option agreements are expected. At Series A, every grant must be documented with a signed agreement, current ATO reporting, and a clear pool forecast for the next 18 months of planned hiring.

## Section 4: Financial History

**Required (Seed):**
- P&L statements (12–24 months, monthly)
- Balance sheet (current)
- Cash flow statement (12–24 months)
- Bank statements (last 3 months)
- ATO Business Activity Statements (BAS) — last 4 quarters
- Tax returns (last 1–2 years)

**Required (Series A additions):**
- Audited financial statements (last 2 years minimum)
- Management accounts (current year, monthly)
- Revenue recognition policy
- Deferred revenue schedule

**Critical AU-specific check:** Investors will cross-reference your BAS against your P&L. GST-reported revenue and your P&L revenue must reconcile. Unexplained discrepancies are a due diligence red flag that adds weeks to close.

## Section 5: Financial Model and Projections

**Required documents:**
- 18–24 month financial model (P&L, cash flow, headcount plan)
- 3–5 year strategic model
- Unit economics summary (CAC, LTV, payback period, gross margin)
- Scenario analysis (base, bull, bear)
- Key assumptions log with sources

**The assumptions log is not optional.** Every model assumption must be sourced. "Industry standard 20% churn" is not a source. "Based on 12 months of internal cohort data — Q1 2026 cohort: 18% annualised churn" is. Investors are forensic about assumptions because assumptions are where model manipulation happens.

## Section 6: R&D Tax Incentive Records

This section is AU-specific and the most commonly overlooked by first-time founders. The **Australian R&D Tax Incentive** returns up to 43.5 cents in the dollar for eligible R&D expenditure for companies under A$20M turnover. For a startup spending A$500K per year on product development, that is potentially A$217K in non-dilutive cash back.

**Required documents:**
- AusIndustry R&D registration confirmation (current year and prior years)
- ATO R&D tax offset claims lodged
- Description of eligible R&D activities (as submitted to AusIndustry)
- R&D expenditure schedule by financial year

**If you have not claimed the R&D Tax Incentive:** Flag it proactively in the data room as an identified opportunity. Investors respond positively to founders who demonstrate commercial awareness of non-dilutive funding sources.

## Section 7: Legal Agreements and Contracts

**Required documents:**
- Customer contracts (MSAs, subscription agreements, SOWs)
- Key vendor and supplier agreements
- Employment contracts (all current employees)
- Contractor agreements with IP assignment clauses highlighted
- Advisory agreements with equity terms
- Insurance policies (D&O, professional indemnity, cyber)

**D&O insurance timeline:** Directors and Officers insurance is expected at Series A. Some institutional investors require confirmation that D&O coverage is in place before they will join your board. Do not wait until due diligence to find this is a gap.

## Section 8: Intellectual Property

**Required documents:**
- IP assignment deeds from founders and all early contractors to the company
- Patent applications or grants (AU and international)
- Trademark registrations (IP Australia)
- Domain name registrations
- Open source software inventory with licence types
- Software architecture summary (high-level)

**The pre-incorporation IP problem:** IP developed by founders before the company was incorporated — and never formally assigned — is the most common dealbreaker in Australian startup M&A and Series A due diligence. Run an IP audit before you open the data room. If there is unassigned IP, fix it with IP assignment deeds before the first investor request.

## Common Australian Data Room Mistakes

**Missing R&D Tax Incentive documentation.** If you have been investing in product, you almost certainly have claimable R&D expenditure. Not capturing this is leaving real cash on the table.

**Unsigned SHA.** A Shareholders Agreement unsigned by any party is a legal document that does not exist. Investors will not proceed past due diligence until this is resolved.

**Cap table discrepancy.** Your cap table must match your ASIC shareholder register, your ESOP register, and your SHA — exactly. Any discrepancy between these three sources triggers a due diligence pause.

**Customer contracts without IP assignment.** If your product was built using customer-funded custom development, and the contracts do not clearly assign IP back to your company, investors will require a legal opinion before proceeding.

**No version control.** Sharing a data room via Google Drive with no access control or version tracking is an operational red flag. Use a purpose-built data room tool that allows section-level sharing and document version history.
