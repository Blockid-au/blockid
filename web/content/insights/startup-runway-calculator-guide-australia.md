## Extend Your Startup Runway: Calculator & Tips for Australian Founders

As an Australian founder, managing your startup's runway is crucial to ensuring the longevity and success of your business. In this article, we'll explore the importance of calculating your startup runway, provide a simple formula to do so, and offer actionable tips to extend your runway.

### What is Startup Runway?

Startup runway refers to the amount of time your business has to operate before it runs out of funds. It's a critical metric that helps you plan and make informed decisions about your company's financial future.

## Calculating Your Startup Runway

The formula to calculate your startup runway is straightforward:

Runway = Cash / Monthly Burn

Where:

* Cash is your current available cash balance
* Monthly Burn is your average monthly expenses

For example, if you have $100,000 in cash and your monthly burn is $10,000, your runway would be:

Runway = $100,000 / $10,000 = 10 months

### Benchmarks for Australian Startups

According to industry benchmarks, it's recommended to raise funds when you have 12+ months of runway left. This allows you to have a safety net and avoid last-minute fundraising, which can be stressful and often result in unfavorable terms.

> **"The general rule of thumb is to raise money when you have 12-18 months of runway left. This gives you enough time to secure a good deal and avoid being in a position of desperation."** - [Startup Genome](https://www.startupgenome.com/)

## Extend Your Startup Runway: Tips and Strategies

Extending your startup runway requires a combination of reducing expenses and increasing revenue. Here are some actionable tips to help you do so:

### Reduce Expenses

| Strategy | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Reduce salaries | Consider reducing founder salaries or deferring pay until later |
| Kill low-ROI channels | Identify and eliminate marketing channels with low return on investment |
| Negotiate vendor payment terms | Negotiate with vendors to extend payment terms or offer discounts |

### Increase Revenue

| Strategy | Description |
| --- | --- |
| R&D tax offset timing | Claim R&D tax offsets to reduce tax liabilities and increase cash flow |
| Revenue-based bridge | Consider revenue-based financing options to bridge the gap to profitability |

> **"Australian startups can claim up to 43.5% of R&D expenses as a tax offset. This can provide a significant boost to cash flow and help extend runway."** - [ATO](https://www.ato.gov.au/Business/Research-and-development/Research-and-development-tax-offset/)
