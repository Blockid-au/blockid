## Register a Startup in Australia: ASIC Company Registration Guide

Registering a startup in Australia can be a daunting task, especially for first-time founders. With various business structures to choose from, it's essential to make informed decisions to set your startup up for success. In this guide, we'll walk you through the ASIC company registration process, compare popular business structures, and highlight common mistakes to avoid.

### Choosing the Right Business Structure

When starting a business in Australia, you'll need to decide on a business structure. The most common options for startups are:

| Business Structure | Description | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Pty Ltd (Private Limited Company)** | A separate legal entity from its owners, with shareholders and directors. | Limited liability, tax benefits, scalable | Higher setup costs, regulatory requirements |
| **Discretionary Trust** | A trust where the trustee has discretion over distributions to beneficiaries. | Flexibility, tax benefits, asset protection | Complexity, potential for disputes |
| **Unit Trust** | A trust where beneficiaries (unit holders) receive distributions based on their unit holdings. | Flexibility, scalability, tax benefits | Complexity, potential for disputes |

> **"The choice of business structure can have significant implications for your startup's growth, funding, and tax obligations. It's essential to consider your goals, risk tolerance, and scalability requirements."**

For most startups, a **Pty Ltd** structure is recommended due to its scalability, limited liability, and tax benefits.

### ASIC Company Registration

To register a Pty Ltd company with ASIC, you'll need to:

1. **Check business name availability**: Ensure your desired business name is available using ASIC's business name checker.
2. **Prepare required documents**: You'll need to provide:
	* Company name and address
	* Director and shareholder information
	* Share capital and dividend information
3. **Lodge your application**: Submit your application online through ASIC's website or through a registered agent.

The registration fee for a Pty Ltd company is **$576** (online).

### Getting an ABN and ACN

As a registered company, you'll need to obtain an **Australian Business Number (ABN)** and **Australian Company Number (ACN)**.

* **ABN**: Apply through the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) website.
* **ACN**: Your ACN will be assigned during the ASIC registration process.

### Director Duties from Day One

As a director of a Pty Ltd company, you'll have various duties and responsibilities, including:

* **Acting in the best interests of the company**
* **Exercising due care and diligence**
* **Maintaining accurate financial records**

### Setting Up for ESIC Eligibility

To be eligible for the Australian Government's **Early Stage Innovation Company (ESIC)** program, your startup must meet specific criteria, including:

* **Having an annual turnover of $200 million or less**
* **Having a balance sheet value of $500 million or less**
* **Being at risk of investing in innovation**

> **Did you know that ESIC-eligible startups can access up to $200 million in investor funding?** 

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

* **Registering as a sole trader when raising equity**: This can lead to significant tax and regulatory issues down the line.
* **Not seeking professional advice**: Consult with a lawyer, accountant, or business advisor to ensure you're making informed decisions.

### Conclusion

Registering a startup in Australia requires careful consideration of business structures, regulatory requirements, and tax obligations. By choosing the right business structure, registering correctly with ASIC, and setting up for ESIC eligibility, you can position your startup for growth and success.
