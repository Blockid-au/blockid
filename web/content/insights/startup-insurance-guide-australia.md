## Startup Insurance Australia: Essential Coverage for Founders

As an Australian startup founder, navigating the complex world of insurance can be daunting. However, having the right coverage in place is crucial to protect your business, team, and investors. In this guide, we'll break down the essential insurance covers for startups at different stages, from pre-seed to Series A, and provide actionable advice on costs, providers, and VC term sheet requirements.

### Pre-Seed Stage Insurance ( $0 - $500k revenue)

At the pre-seed stage, your primary focus is on developing your product or service and securing initial funding. However, it's essential to have some basic insurance covers in place to mitigate potential risks.

| **Insurance Type** | **Description** | **Recommended Coverage** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public Liability | Covers damages to third parties, including customers, suppliers, or bystanders | $2 million |
| Cyber Insurance | Protects against cyber threats, data breaches, and hacking | $1 million |

> "64% of Australian businesses reported a cyber attack in 2022, with the average cost of a breach being $39,000." - Australian Cyber Security Centre

At this stage, consider the following providers:

* **Startup Shield**: Offers tailored insurance solutions for early-stage startups, including public liability and cyber insurance.
* **Agile Underwriting**: Provides flexible, on-demand insurance policies for startups, including cyber and public liability coverage.

### Seed Stage Insurance ( $500k - $2 million revenue)

As your startup grows, you'll need to consider additional insurance covers to protect your business, team, and investors.

| **Insurance Type** | **Description** | **Recommended Coverage** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Directors & Officers (D&O) Insurance | Protects directors and officers against claims of negligence, breach of duty, or mismanagement | $2 million |
| Professional Indemnity (PI) Insurance | Covers damages resulting from professional negligence or breach of contract | $2 million |

D&O insurance is critical at this stage, as it protects your directors and officers from personal liability. PI insurance is also essential, as it covers damages resulting from professional negligence or breach of contract.

### Series A Stage Insurance ( $2 million+ revenue)

At the Series A stage, your startup is growing rapidly, and you'll need to consider more comprehensive insurance covers.

| **Insurance Type** | **Description** | **Recommended Coverage** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Key Man Insurance | Covers the loss of a key employee or director | $2 million |
| Employment Practices Liability (EPL) Insurance | Protects against employee claims of harassment, discrimination, or unfair dismissal | $2 million |
| Product Liability Insurance | Covers damages resulting from defective products | $5 million |

### Costs and Providers

The cost of startup insurance in Australia varies widely depending on the type and level of coverage. Here are some estimated costs:

| **Insurance Type** | **Estimated Cost** |
| --- | --- |
| Public Liability | $500 - $2,000 per year |
| Cyber Insurance | $1,000 - $5,000 per year |
| D&O Insurance | $2,000 - $10,000 per year |
| PI Insurance | $1,500 - $5,000 per year |

Some popular providers for startup insurance in Australia include:

* **Embroker**: Offers a range of insurance products, including D&O, PI, and cyber insurance.
* **Startup Shield**: Provides tailored insurance solutions for early-stage startups.
* **Agile Underwriting**: Offers flexible, on-demand insurance policies for startups.

### VC Term Sheet Requirements

When raising capital from venture capital investors, you'll often be required to have certain insurance covers in place. These may include:

* D&O insurance
* PI insurance
* Cyber insurance
* Key man insurance

### Common Claims Startups Face

Startups often face unique risks and claims. Some common claims include:

* Cyber breaches and data losses
* Professional negligence and breach of contract claims
* Employee disputes and unfair dismissal claims
* Product liability claims

### Conclusion

In conclusion, having the right insurance coverage is essential for Australian startups at every stage. By understanding the essential covers for pre-seed, seed, and Series A stages, you can protect your business, team, and investors from potential risks.
