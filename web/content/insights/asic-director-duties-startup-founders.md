## ASIC Director Duties Australia: A Startup Founder's Guide

As an Australian startup founder, understanding your director duties under the Corporations Act 2001 is crucial to ensure compliance and avoid personal liabilities. In this guide, we'll walk you through the key duties, liabilities, and best practices for startup directors.

### Key Director Duties Under Corporations Act 2001

As a director of an Australian company, you're required to perform your duties in accordance with the Corporations Act 2001. The key duties include:

| Duty | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Care and Diligence** | Exercise reasonable care and diligence when making decisions. |
| **Good Faith** | Act in good faith and in the best interests of the company. |
| **Proper Purpose** | Act for a proper purpose and not for an improper purpose. |
| **Avoid Conflicts** | Avoid conflicts of interest and not improperly use your position or information. |
| **No Improper Use of Position/Information** | Not improperly use your position or information obtained as a director. |

> **"A director's duties are not just about following the law, but also about acting with integrity and in the best interests of the company."** [Source: ASIC]

### Insolvent Trading and Personal Liability

As a director, you're personally liable for insolvent trading if your company trades while insolvent. Insolvent trading occurs when a company is unable to pay its debts as they fall due.

> **"If you continue to trade while your company is insolvent, you may be personally liable for the debts incurred during that period."** [Source: ASIC]

To avoid insolvent trading, ensure your company maintains accurate financial records and seeks professional advice if you're unsure about its financial position.

### Director and Officer (D&O) Insurance

D&O insurance can protect you and your fellow directors from personal liability. It's recommended to obtain D&O insurance when your startup reaches Series A funding.

### ASIC Director Duties Checklist

Use the following checklist to ensure you're meeting your director duties:

- [ ] Exercise care and diligence when making decisions.
- [ ] Act in good faith and in the best interests of the company.
- [ ] Act for a proper purpose and not for an improper purpose.
- [ ] Avoid conflicts of interest and not improperly use your position or information.
- [ ] Maintain accurate financial records and seek professional advice if unsure about the company's financial position.

### How Founder Duties Change with External Investors

As your startup grows and attracts external investors, your director duties may change. You'll need to balance your duties to the company with your duties to investors.

| **Founder Duties** | **Pre-Investors** | **Post-Investors** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary Focus** | Company growth and success | Company growth, investor returns, and governance compliance |
| **Decision-Making** | Autonomous decision-making | Collaborative decision-making with investors |
| **Risk Management** | Focus on company-specific risks | Focus on company-specific and investor-related risks |
