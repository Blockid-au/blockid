## Co-Founder Agreement Template for Australian Startups: What to Include

More Australian startups fall apart from co-founder disputes than from market failure. A co-founder agreement — sometimes called a founders' agreement or shareholders' agreement — is the legal document that prevents that from happening. Get it wrong and you risk a founder walkout, equity disputes, and a company that investors won't touch.

This guide covers exactly what an Australian **co-founder agreement** must include, the legal mechanisms that protect all parties, and the equity mistakes that kill startups before they launch.

### Why a Co-Founder Agreement is Non-Negotiable

Under the Corporations Act 2001, a company incorporated with two directors and equal shares has no default mechanism for resolving disputes. Without a co-founder agreement, you are at the mercy of:

- **Deadlock provisions** — two 50/50 shareholders can block every decision
- **Departing founder retaining full equity** — even after they leave
- **IP ownership ambiguity** — if code was written before incorporation, who owns it?
- **Investor red flags** — no co-founder agreement at due diligence is an immediate concern for Australian VCs

### The 6 Essential Clauses

#### 1. Equity Split and Rationale

Document how you reached your equity split — even if it's 50/50. Investors want to see that you've had the uncomfortable conversation and reached consensus.

**Common splits:**
- 50/50: Equal; only sustainable with a pre-agreed deadlock resolution mechanism
- 60/40: Recognises a lead founder; cleaner governance
- Weighted by role: Technical founder / commercial founder split by relative value

> **AU Best Practice:** Document the equity split *and* the methodology. "Equal contribution" is fine. "Technical co-founder built the core IP" is fine. Vague or undocumented is not fine.

#### 2. Vesting Schedule (the most critical clause)

Vesting ensures that equity is earned over time — protecting the company if a co-founder leaves early.

**Standard Australian VC-accepted vesting:**

| Term | Standard |
|---|---|
| Total vest period | 4 years |
| Cliff | 12 months (nothing vests until month 12) |
| Vesting cadence | Monthly after cliff |
| Acceleration | Single trigger (25% on acquisition) or double trigger |

**Why it matters:** Without vesting, a co-founder who exits after 6 months keeps 50% of the company. With a 4-year vest and 1-year cliff, they leave with 0%.

#### 3. IP Assignment

All intellectual property — code, designs, trademarks, trade secrets — created in connection with the startup must be assigned to the company, not remain with individual founders.

**IP assignment must cover:**
- Code written before and after incorporation
- Domain names, social handles, trademarks
- Customer data and relationships
- Inventions conceived but not yet built

Without IP assignment, a departing co-founder could claim ownership of core technology and demand a licence fee — or block your fundraising entirely.

#### 4. Roles, Responsibilities, and Commitment Level

**Define:**
- Title and functional area (CEO, CTO, CPO, etc.)
- Full-time vs part-time commitment (and threshold at which equity adjusts)
- Decision-making authority by domain (engineering decisions vs commercial decisions vs hiring)
- Salary (often $0 until funding, but document the agreement)

#### 5. Dispute Resolution

Include a tiered dispute resolution mechanism before escalating to litigation:

1. Good-faith negotiation (14 days)
2. Mediation via LEADR or ACDC (30 days)
3. If deadlock on a major decision: CEO/lead founder casts deciding vote, or buy-out mechanism triggers

#### 6. Exit Provisions (Buyout and Drag-Along)

**What happens if a founder leaves?**
- **Good leaver** (illness, death, mutual agreement): Unvested shares lapse; vested shares retained or bought back at fair market value
- **Bad leaver** (termination for cause): Unvested AND vested shares subject to buyback at nominal value

**Drag-along:** If 75%+ of shareholders approve an exit, they can force minority shareholders to sell at the same terms. This is essential for a clean M&A exit.

**Tag-along:** If a majority shareholder sells, minority shareholders get the right to sell too at the same price.

### Pre-Incorporation Checklist

Before signing anything:

- [ ] Have the equity conversation — document the split in writing
- [ ] Assign all existing IP to the company on day 1
- [ ] Set 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff for all founders
- [ ] Agree on lead decision-maker when founders disagree
- [ ] Define what "full-time" means and consequences of part-time work
- [ ] Document salary (even if $0 until funded)
- [ ] Set a cadence for formal board/director meetings (quarterly minimum)

### Red Flags That Kill Fundraising

| Red Flag | Why Investors Care |
|---|---|
| No co-founder agreement | Can't verify clean IP ownership |
| Equal equity, no vesting | Founder departure = company collapse |
| IP not assigned to entity | Due diligence fatal |
| No dispute resolution clause | Single dispute can dissolve company |
| Founder loans with no cap | Creates debt overhang |
