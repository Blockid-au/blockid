## Hiring Your First Employee in Australia: A Startup Guide
As an Australian startup founder, hiring your first employee is a significant milestone. It marks a new phase of growth and brings new challenges, particularly in compliance and employee management. This guide walks you through the essential steps and considerations for hiring your first employee in Australia.

### Understanding ABN vs Employment
Before hiring, it's crucial to understand the difference between engaging a contractor (using an Australian Business Number or ABN) and employing a staff member. The key distinction lies in control and direction over the work performed.

| **ABN (Contractor)** | **Employment** |
| --- | --- |
| Independent operator, provides own equipment | Employer provides equipment, directs work |
| Responsible for own taxes and super | Employer responsible for PAYG withholding and super guarantee |
| No entitlement to annual leave, sick leave | Entitled to annual leave, sick leave, and other benefits |

> **Did you know?** Misclassifying an employee as a contractor can lead to significant penalties. Ensure you understand the Fair Work Act obligations.

### Setting Up PAYG Withholding
As an employer, you're required to withhold tax from your employee's salary and pay it to the ATO. You'll need to:

1. Register for PAYG withholding
2. Obtain a PAYG withholding certificate
3. Withhold tax according to the ATO's tax tables

### Super Guarantee and Payroll Tax
As an employer, you're required to pay a super guarantee of **11.5%** (increasing to 12% in 2027) of your employee's base salary. Additionally, you'll need to consider payroll tax thresholds, which vary by state.

| **State** | **Payroll Tax Threshold** | **Payroll Tax Rate** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| NSW | $1,000,000 | 4.85% |
| VIC | $1,000,000 | 4.15% |
| QLD | $1,500,000 | 4.75% |
| WA | $1,000,000 | 4.5% |
| SA | $1,500,000 | 4.95% |
| TAS | $1,500,000 | 4% |
| ACT | $1,000,000 | 4.5% |
| NT | $1,500,000 | 5.5% |

### Equity vs Salary: When to Offer Equity
When hiring your first employee, you may consider offering equity instead of a higher salary. This can be an attractive option, but it's essential to understand the implications on your cap table.

> **Consider this:** Offering equity can dilute your ownership, but it can also align your employee's interests with the company's success.

### Common Mistakes Founders Make
When hiring their first employee, founders often make the following mistakes:

- Misclassifying employees as contractors
- Failing to register for PAYG withholding
- Not paying super guarantee
- Not understanding Fair Work Act obligations

### Option Pool Sizing and Cap Table Impact
When offering equity to employees, it's crucial to consider the impact on your cap table. A well-structured option pool can help you attract and retain top talent.

> **Did you know?** A 1% option pool can dilute your ownership by 1%, but it can also provide a significant incentive for your employees.

### Fair Work Act Obligations
As an employer, you're required to comply with the Fair Work Act, which includes:

- Providing a safe work environment
- Paying employees correctly
- Providing leave and other benefits

### Conclusion
Hiring your first employee in Australia requires careful consideration of various factors, including ABN vs employment, PAYG withholding, super guarantee, payroll tax, and Fair Work Act obligations. By understanding these requirements and making informed decisions, you can set your startup up for success.
