## Understanding Pre-Revenue Startup Valuation

Valuing a pre-revenue startup can be challenging, as it requires assessing the company's potential for growth and returns on investment. In Australia, pre-seed startups typically raise between **$1-4 million**, while seed-stage startups raise between **$3-10 million**.

### Australian Startup Valuation Benchmarks

| Stage | Valuation Range (AUD) |
| --- | --- |
| Pre-Seed | $1-4 million |
| Seed | $3-10 million |
| Series A | $10-20 million |

## 5 Methods for Valuing Pre-Revenue Startups

### 1. Berkus Method

The Berkus Method assesses a startup's value based on five key areas:

*   **Idea**: The uniqueness and potential of the startup's idea
*   **Prototype**: The development and feasibility of the product or service
*   **Team**: The experience and expertise of the founding team
*   **Execution**: The startup's ability to execute its business plan
*   **Market**: The size and growth potential of the target market

Each area is scored from $0 to $500,000, with a maximum total valuation of **$2.5 million**.

### 2. Scorecard Method

The Scorecard Method compares a startup to similar companies in the same region and industry. The valuation is based on a weighted score of factors such as:

*   **Team**: 20%
*   **Product/Prototype**: 20%
*   **Market**: 20%
*   **Sales and Traction**: 20%
*   **Partnerships and Advisors**: 10%
*   **Environment (Market Conditions)**: 10%

> **"The Scorecard Method provides a more nuanced view of a startup's valuation by considering multiple factors and comparing it to similar companies."** 

### 3. Risk Factor Summation Method

The Risk Factor Summation Method assesses a startup's valuation by identifying and scoring various risk factors, such as:

*   **Management Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Stage of Business Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Legislative/Political Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Manufacturing Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Sales and Marketing Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Financial Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Competition Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Technology Risk**: 0-5%
*   **Litigation Risk**: 0-5%
*   **International Risk**: 0-5%

The base valuation is adjusted based on these risk factors.

### 4. VC Method

The VC Method estimates a startup's valuation based on the expected exit value and the required return on investment. This method involves:

1.  Estimating the exit value (e.g., acquisition or IPO)
2.  Determining the required return on investment (e.g., 10x return)
3.  Calculating the pre-money valuation

### 5. Comparable Transactions Method

The Comparable Transactions Method involves analyzing recent transactions of similar startups to determine a fair valuation.
