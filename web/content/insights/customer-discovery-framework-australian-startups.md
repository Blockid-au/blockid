## Customer Discovery Framework for Australian Startups: Validate Before You Build

The most expensive mistake an Australian founder can make is building a product nobody wants. Customer discovery — the systematic process of testing assumptions before writing a line of code — is the antidote. Yet most founders skip it entirely, or do it so superficially it provides false validation.

This guide gives you a concrete **customer discovery framework** tailored to the Australian market, covering the tools, scripts, and metrics that separate founders who build things people want from those who build things that go to zero.

### Why Australian Founders Need a Different Approach

The Australian startup ecosystem has unique characteristics that affect customer discovery:

- **Smaller TAM**: Australia has 25M people — customer segments are smaller and easier to exhaust
- **Cultural directness**: Australians give blunt feedback more readily than US counterparts (use this advantage)
- **LinkedIn adoption**: AU professionals respond better to LinkedIn outreach than cold email
- **Government co-validation**: Programs like ATO R&D Tax and Accelerating Commercialisation validate market fit for investors

### Phase 1: Problem Discovery (Weeks 1–3)

Before building anything, you must validate that a real problem exists — and that people care enough to pay to solve it.

**Goal:** 20 problem interviews with target customers

**Who to interview:**
- Your top 5 industry contacts (warm)
- LinkedIn 1st/2nd connections in your ICP (cold outreach)
- Members of relevant Startup Vic, Stone & Chalk, or industry association communities

**The Mum Test Script (adapted for AU):**

1. *"Can you walk me through how you currently handle [problem area]?"*
2. *"What's the most frustrating part of that process?"*
3. *"How much time/money does that cost you per month?"*
4. *"What have you already tried to fix it?"*
5. *"If you could wave a magic wand and fix this tomorrow, what would that look like?"*

> **Critical rule:** Never mention your solution. You're listening for their pain, not pitching your answer.

**What good looks like:** At least 60% of interviewees describe the same top-3 frustrations without prompting.

### Phase 2: Solution Discovery (Weeks 4–6)

Once you've validated the problem exists, test whether your proposed solution resonates — still without building it.

**Tools:**
- Figma or Canva mockups (lo-fi is fine)
- A Notion or Carrd landing page with email signup
- Typeform survey sent to your problem interview list

**Concierge MVP:** Manually deliver the outcome your product will eventually automate. If you're building financial modelling software, do the modelling manually in spreadsheets for 3–5 pilot customers. This validates willingness to pay without a single line of code.

**Signal thresholds:**
| Signal | Weak | Strong |
|---|---|---|
| Landing page conversion | < 5% email signup | > 15% email signup |
| Concierge pilots | < 2 willing | ≥ 5 paying for manual service |
| WTP (willingness to pay) | "Maybe someday" | Names a price and timeline |
| Re-engagement | No follow-up | Emails YOU asking for update |

### Phase 3: MVP + Early Metrics (Weeks 7–12)

Build the smallest version that lets you measure whether customers achieve the outcome they care about.

**The right MVP metrics for AU startups:**

| Stage | Metric | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-revenue | Activation rate (% who complete core action) | > 40% |
| Early revenue | Week-1 retention | > 30% |
| Product-market fit signal | NPS | > 40 |
| PMF confirmation | Retention curve flattens | Month-3 retention > 20% |

**AU-specific distribution channels for early MVPs:**
- **Startup communities:** Startup Vic, Sydney Startup Hub, Startmate alumni network
- **Accelerators as distribution:** Antler, Startmate, QUT Creative Enterprise Australia
- **LinkedIn + Canva/Atlassian alumni networks:** Rich source of product-savvy early adopters
- **ATO innovation communities:** For fintech/regtech founders

### Common Mistakes AU Founders Make

1. **Interviewing friends and family** — they'll validate anything to be supportive
2. **Pitching instead of listening** — you learn nothing when you're talking
3. **Asking hypothetical questions** — "Would you use this?" is worthless; "Have you ever tried to solve this?" is gold
4. **Skipping the enterprise channel** — Australian B2B customers are often more accessible than US ones; use it
5. **Ignoring the sales conversation** — the moment someone asks "how much does it cost?" you've validated the problem

### The Validation Stack for AU Founders

Combine customer discovery with these Australian validation pathways:

- **R&D Tax Incentive** — 43.5% cash rebate on eligible development costs (ATO); running a structured discovery process strengthens your R&D claim
- **Accelerating Commercialisation** — AusIndustry grant up to A$1M; requires evidence of commercial validation
- **Antler / Startmate** — Both programs run structured validation sprints; acceptance validates your process rigor
