## The 13-Section Data Room Framework

### Section 1: Company Formation and ASIC Documents

This is the legal foundation. Every document in this section answers one question: does this company legally exist and is it properly structured?

**Required documents:**
- ASIC Certificate of Registration (ABN/ACN confirmation)
- Company Constitution (or confirmation of replaceable rules adoption)
- Current ASIC Company Extract (shows company history, name changes, director history)
- Evidence of annual ASIC review fee payments (last 2 years minimum)
- ABN registration confirmation (ATO)
- GST registration (if applicable — mandatory above A$75K revenue)

**AU-specific note:** International investors unfamiliar with Australia may not recognise ASIC documents. Include a one-paragraph explanatory note in each section header: "ASIC is Australia's corporate regulator, equivalent to Companies House (UK) or the SEC (US)."

### Section 2: Cap Table and Equity Structure

The cap table is the single most scrutinised document in any Australian due diligence process. Investors need to understand exactly who owns what, on what terms, and what happens to the ownership structure post-investment.

**Required documents:**
- Fully diluted cap table (current and post-investment scenarios)
- Founder share certificates
- Shareholder register (current, stamped)
- Shareholders Agreement (SHA) — complete, signed by all parties
- Founder vesting schedules (with any acceleration provisions noted)
- SAFE / convertible note agreements (if applicable — include conversion mechanics)
- ESIC eligibility documentation (if applicable)

**AU-specific note:** Australian investors will specifically check whether your company meets **Early Stage Innovation Company (ESIC)** criteria, as this entitles their investors to a 20% tax offset. If you qualify, document this explicitly. A ESIC eligibility checklist and ATO confirmation letter belong in this section.

### Section 3: ESOP Documentation

The Employee Share Option Plan deserves its own section, not a subfolder buried under legal. Investors at seed and Series A spend significant time on ESOP structure because a non-compliant or undersized pool creates dilution issues post-investment.

**Required documents:**
- ESS deed (complete, board-approved, with execution date)
- Individual option agreements for each current optionholder
- Option holder register (separate from shareholder register)
- ATO ESS Annual Report lodgements (last 1–2 years)
- ESOP pool summary: total pool size, granted, vested, available
- Good leaver / bad leaver provisions summary

**Completion benchmark:** If your ESOP section is missing the ATO annual report or individual option agreements are unsigned, flag this proactively and provide a remediation timeline. Investors respond better to transparency than to discovering gaps themselves.

### Section 4: Financials — Historical

**Required documents (Seed):**
- P&L statements (12–24 months, monthly)
- Balance sheet (current, audited if possible)
- Cash flow statement (12–24 months)
- Bank statements (last 3 months)
- ATO Business Activity Statements (BAS) — last 4 quarters
- Tax returns (last 1–2 years, if filed)

**Required documents (Series A — additional):**
- Audited financial statements (last 2 years minimum)
- Management accounts (current year, monthly)
- Revenue recognition policy documentation
- Deferred revenue schedule (if applicable)

**AU-specific note:** Australian investors will cross-reference your BAS with your P&L. Inconsistencies between GST-reported revenue and your P&L are a major red flag. Ensure these reconcile before sharing.

### Section 5: Financial Model and Projections

**Required documents:**
- 18–24 month financial model (P&L, cash flow, headcount)
- 3–5 year strategic model (Series A and beyond)
- Unit economics summary (CAC, LTV, payback period, gross margin)
- Scenario analysis (base, bull, bear)
- Key assumptions log

**AU-specific note:** Australian founders often underestimate how forensically investors examine model assumptions. Every assumption must be sourced. "Industry standard" is not a source. "Based on 6 months of internal data / competitor pricing disclosed in X" is.

### Section 6: R&D Tax Incentive Records

This section is AU-specific and frequently overlooked by first-time founders. The **Australian R&D Tax Incentive** (administered jointly by ATO and AusIndustry) can return 43.5 cents in the dollar for eligible R&D expenditure for companies with under A$20M turnover. This is a material non-dilutive funding source that investors want to see you are capturing.

**Required documents:**
- AusIndustry R&D registration confirmation (current year and prior years)
- ATO R&D tax offset claims lodged
- Description of eligible R&D activities (as submitted)
- R&D expenditure schedule by financial year

If you have not claimed the R&D Tax Incentive and have eligible activities, flag this to investors as an identified opportunity. It demonstrates commercial awareness.

### Section 7: Legal Agreements and Key Contracts

**Required documents:**
- All customer contracts (MSAs, SaaS subscription agreements, SOWs)
- Key vendor and supplier agreements
- Employment contracts (all current employees — redacted for salary detail if preferred)
- Contractor agreements (IP assignment clauses highlighted)
- Advisory agreements (with equity compensation disclosed)
- Insurance policies (D&O, PI, cyber — critical for Series A)

**AU-specific note:** Under the *Privacy Act 1988*, your customer contracts must include compliant data handling provisions. If your contracts pre-date the 2022 Privacy Act reforms, they may need updating. Flag any known gaps.

### Section 8: Intellectual Property

**Required documents:**
- IP assignment deeds (from founders and early contractors to the company)
- Patent applications or granted patents (AU and international)
- Trademark registrations (IP Australia)
- Domain name registrations
- Open source software inventory (with licence types — GPL exposure is a red flag)
- Software architecture summary (high-level; not source code)

**Common mistake:** Founder IP that was developed before company incorporation and never formally assigned. This is a dealbreaker. Run an IP audit before opening your data room.

### Section 9: Team and Organisation

**Required documents:**
- Founder bios (professional background, domain expertise, LinkedIn profiles)
- Full team roster with roles, start dates, and employment status
- Organisation chart (current and 12-month planned)
- Hiring plan (linked to financial model headcount assumptions)
- Advisor profiles with engagement terms
- Board composition and minutes (last 4 board meetings)

### Section 10: Product and Technology

**Required documents:**
- Product overview / one-pager
- Technical architecture summary
- Product roadmap (12–18 months)
- Security and compliance certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001 if applicable)
- Uptime / reliability metrics
- Key technology dependencies and risks

### Section 11: Market and Competitive Analysis

**Required documents:**
- TAM/SAM/SOM analysis with sources
- Competitive landscape matrix
- Customer segmentation and ICP definition
- Market timing rationale
- Regulatory environment analysis (AU-specific)

### Section 12: Traction and Customer Evidence

This section converts claims into evidence. Investors discount founder assertions by default — they trust third-party validation.

**Required documents:**
- Revenue metrics dashboard (MRR/ARR trend, churn, NRR)
- Customer list (with permission to disclose, or anonymised by tier)
- NPS scores or customer satisfaction data
- Case studies / customer testimonials (written or video)
- Letters of Intent (for pre-revenue companies)
- Pilot agreement terms and outcomes
- Press coverage and third-party mentions

### Section 13: Pitch Materials and Strategic Documents

**Required documents:**
- Current investor pitch deck (PDF)
- Executive summary (2-page maximum)
- Investment thesis / why now narrative
- Use of funds breakdown
- Previous investor communications (if applicable)
- Cap table model (post-investment scenarios)

## Stage-Appropriate Completion Benchmarks

| Stage | Minimum Completion | Critical Sections | Estimated Setup Time |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :---: |
| Pre-Seed / Angel | 50–60% | Sections 1, 2, 9, 13 | 1–2 weeks |
| Seed | 70–80% | Sections 1–5, 9–11, 13 | 2–4 weeks |
| Series A | 85–95% | All 13 sections | 4–8 weeks |

**The 70% rule for seed:** You do not need a perfect data room to start investor conversations. You need a complete data room to close. The practical approach is to open conversations with 50–60% completion, but sprint to 70%+ before any investor enters formal due diligence.

## Common Australian Data Room Mistakes

**1. Missing R&D Tax Incentive documentation.** This is the most commonly overlooked AU-specific requirement. If you have been investing in product development, you almost certainly have claimable R&D expenditure.

**2. Unsigned SHA.** A Shareholders Agreement that has not been signed by all parties — including any early investors — is a legal landmine. Investors will not proceed until it is resolved.

**3. Cap table discrepancies.** If your cap table does not match your ASIC shareholder register, your ESOP register, and your SHA — these three documents must reconcile exactly — you will lose weeks fixing it during due diligence.

**4. Customer contracts without IP assignment clauses.** If your product was built using customer-funded development and the contracts do not clearly assign IP back to the company, investors will require legal opinions before proceeding.

**5. No D&O insurance.** Directors and Officers insurance is expected at Series A. Not having it in place is a gap some investors will require you to close before closing the round.
