## Australian Startup Equity Benchmarks: Early Employee Allocation Guide

As an Australian startup founder, one of the most critical decisions you'll make is how much equity to allocate to your early employees. Getting this right can make all the difference in attracting and retaining top talent, while also ensuring you have enough equity left for future hires and investors. In this guide, we'll explore Australian startup equity benchmarks, cliff and vesting mechanics, and the benefits of the Australian Employee Share Scheme (ESS) tax concession.

### Understanding Equity Allocation

When it comes to equity allocation, there's no one-size-fits-all approach. However, here are some general benchmarks to consider:

| Role | Equity Allocation |
| --- | --- |
| Engineer | 0.1-0.5% |
| Designer | 0.05-0.3% |
| VP/Head | 0.25-1% |
| First 10 Employees | 0.5-2% |
| Employees 11-50 | 0.1-0.5% |

> **"The key is to be fair and competitive, but also to leave room for future growth and investment."** - Australian startup founder

### Cliff and Vesting Mechanics

When it comes to equity allocation, it's essential to consider cliff and vesting mechanics. A cliff period is a specified period during which an employee must remain with the company before they can exercise their stock options. Vesting schedules determine how much equity an employee can exercise over time.

Here's an example of a common vesting schedule:

* 25% cliff after 12 months
* 75% vesting over 3 years

> **"A well-structured vesting schedule can help ensure that employees are incentivized to stay with the company long-term."**

### Australian ESS Tax Concession

The Australian Employee Share Scheme (ESS) tax concession provides a significant benefit to startups and employees. Under the ESS, employees can receive tax-free shares up to a certain limit.

Here are the ESS tax concession benefits:

* Up to **$1,000** tax-free shares per employee per year
* No income tax on shares received under the ESS
* No capital gains tax on shares received under the ESS

> **"The ESS tax concession is a game-changer for Australian startups, allowing us to attract and retain top talent while minimizing tax liabilities."** - Australian startup founder

### Common Founder Mistake

One common mistake founders make is being too generous with equity allocation early on. While it's essential to attract top talent, being too generous can leave you with limited equity for future hires and investors.

> **"Being too generous early on can kill your ability to hire later employees or attract investors. It's essential to strike a balance."** - Australian startup founder
