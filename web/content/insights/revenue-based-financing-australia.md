## Revenue Based Financing Australia: A Non-Dilutive Funding Option for Startups
Revenue Based Financing (RBF) is gaining traction in Australia as a non-dilutive funding option for startups, particularly those in the SaaS (Software as a Service) sector. This funding model allows businesses to access capital without giving up equity, making it an attractive alternative to traditional venture capital or angel investments.

### How Revenue Based Financing Works
In RBF, an investor provides a lump sum of capital to the startup in exchange for a percentage of its monthly revenue until a predetermined repayment amount is reached. The repayment period and percentage of revenue are negotiated upfront.

The typical terms for RBF in Australia are:
| **Advance Multiple** | **Repayment Percentage** | **Repayment Period** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 3-12x MRR | 3-12% of monthly revenue | Until 1.3-1.5x repaid |

MRR stands for Monthly Recurring Revenue, which is a key metric for SaaS businesses.

### Benefits of Revenue Based Financing for Australian Startups
RBF offers several benefits to startups, including:

* **Non-dilutive funding**: No equity is given up, allowing founders to maintain control and ownership
* **Flexible repayment terms**: Repayments are tied to revenue, so cash flow is less strained during periods of slower growth
* **Fast access to capital**: Funding can be secured quickly, often within weeks

### Who is Revenue Based Financing Right For?
RBF is particularly suitable for SaaS startups with:
* **$30K+ MRR**: A established revenue stream with a proven business model
* **>12-month track record**: A demonstrated ability to generate consistent revenue
* **Predictable cash flow**: A stable and growing customer base

### Providers of Revenue Based Financing in Australia
Some notable providers of RBF in Australia include:

* **Clearco**: A global provider of RBF with a presence in Australia
* **Ondeck**: A US-based company offering RBF and other funding options in Australia
* **Moula**: An Australian provider of RBF and other funding options for small businesses

### Comparison with Equity Dilution
To illustrate the benefits of RBF, let's compare the economics with equity dilution.

> **"The average SaaS startup loses 20-30% of its equity in the first round of funding."** - AVCAL

Assuming a SaaS startup with $50K MRR and a $1 million valuation, here are the implications of RBF versus equity dilution:

| **Funding Option** | **Capital Raised** | **Cost of Capital** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| RBF (6x MRR) | $300K | 6-12% of monthly revenue until $390K repaid |
| Equity Dilution (20% equity) | $200K (assuming 20% equity stake) | 20% ownership stake |

In this example, RBF provides $300K in capital with a repayment amount that is 1.3x the advance. In contrast, equity dilution results in a 20% ownership stake.

### How to Value a SaaS Startup in Australia
Valuing a SaaS startup in Australia involves considering several factors, including:

* **Revenue growth rate**: A high and consistent growth rate increases valuation
* **Customer acquisition cost (CAC)**: A low CAC and high customer lifetime value (CLV) are attractive to investors
* **Market size and competition**: A large and growing market with limited competition increases valuation

> **"The average valuation multiple for SaaS startups is 5-10x ARR."** - Startup Genome
