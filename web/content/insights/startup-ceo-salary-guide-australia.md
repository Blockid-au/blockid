## Startup CEO Salary Australia: Fair Pay Benchmarks & Tax Tips

As an Australian startup founder, determining your salary can be a complex and sensitive issue. You need to balance your personal financial needs with the expectations of your investors, board members, and the company's financial situation. In this article, we'll provide fair pay benchmarks for startup CEOs in Australia, discuss the tension between founder salary and investor expectations, and offer tax-efficient strategies for remuneration.

### Benchmarking Founder Salaries in Australia

The salary of a startup CEO in Australia varies significantly depending on the stage of the company. Here are some general benchmarks:

| Stage | Salary Range (AUD) |
| --- | --- |
| Pre-Seed | $0 - $80,000 |
| Seed | $80,000 - $150,000 |
| Series A | $150,000 - $250,000 |

These ranges are based on industry reports, surveys, and data from Australian startup ecosystems. Keep in mind that these are general guidelines, and the right salary for you will depend on your company's specific circumstances.

### The Tension Between Founder Salary and Investor Expectations

As a founder, you may face pressure from investors to keep your salary low, as they want to ensure that their investment is being used efficiently to drive growth. However, you also need to ensure that your salary is fair and reflects your contributions to the company.

> **"The key is to find a balance between being frugal and being fair. You don't want to underpay yourself and risk burnout, but you also don't want to overpay yourself and alienate your investors."** - [Australian Startup Founder]

### Justifying Founder Salary in Board Discussions

When discussing your salary with your board, it's essential to be prepared and transparent. Here are some tips:

* Be clear about your role and responsibilities as a founder and CEO.
* Provide a detailed breakdown of your expenses and financial needs.
* Explain how your salary will impact your motivation and ability to drive growth.
* Be open to feedback and willing to adjust your salary based on the company's performance.

### Tax-Efficient Founder Remuneration in Australia

As an Australian founder, you need to consider the tax implications of your salary. Here are some tax-efficient strategies to consider:

* **Salary vs Dividends**: Paying yourself a salary can be tax-efficient, but taking dividends can also be beneficial. Dividends are taxed at a lower rate than salary, but they require the company to have profits.
* **Superannuation**: As a founder, you may not have access to a traditional superannuation scheme. However, you can consider setting up a self-managed super fund (SMSF) to save for retirement.
* **Tax-Efficient Structures**: Consider setting up a company or trust structure that can help minimize tax liabilities.

### When to Increase Salary vs Take Equity

As your company grows, you may need to adjust your salary and equity stake. Here are some general guidelines:

* **Increase Salary**: Consider increasing your salary when the company has achieved significant milestones, such as securing funding or reaching profitability.
* **Take Equity**: Consider taking equity when the company is growing rapidly, and you need to incentivize yourself and your team.
